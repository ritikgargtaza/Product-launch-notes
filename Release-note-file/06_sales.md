# Release Note Prompt — Sales

> **Team context:** Owns new logo acquisition.  
> **KPIs:** Pipeline generated, deal velocity, win rate, ACV.  
> **Orientation:** Needs crisp, sellable narratives — not technical specs.

---

## Team Description (for context)

**Function:**
Responsible for acquiring new merchants and enterprise clients. They need to understand what the product does, who it's for, and how to position it in a sales conversation. They care about pricing, availability, competitive differentiation, and what objections they'll face.

**Key concerns at launch:**
- What is this product and who is the target customer?
- When can we start selling it and to which geographies/segments?
- What is the pricing and commercial model?
- How does this compare to what competitors offer?
- What are the known limitations or constraints we need to disclose to prospects?

**Tone:** Commercial and energising. Make it easy for Sales to tell the story. Lead with the value proposition, then cover constraints clearly so they don't overpromise.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a Senior PMM writing an internal pre-release sync note for the Sales team.

Context about this team: They own new logo acquisition. They need crisp, sellable narratives — not technical specs. Their KPIs include pipeline generated, deal velocity, win rate, and ACV. They want to know: "What can I now say to a prospect that I couldn't say before?"

PRD: [PASTE NOTION/CONFLUENCE LINK]

North star for this release: to sync all teams before the product release — so this note gives them a head start before the release goes live, not a debrief after.

Write a short internal Slack/email note using exactly this structure:

**What's new**
2–3 sentences. The elevator pitch version. One powerful "we now can" statement. Avoid acronyms and technical jargon entirely.

**What's in it for you**
2–3 sentences. Which deals does this unblock? Which competitor objection does it neutralise? Which ICP segment does it open up? Be specific about the commercial angle.

**Inputs required before go-live**
Bullet list (3–5 items max). Examples: updated one-pager or battle card, new talking point for objection handling, revised pricing tier to know, demo environment updated, segment-specific messaging to use.

**Positioning and competitive angle**
3–4 bullet points. Clarify the target customer profile and value prop in sales-friendly language. Highlight competitive differentiation (what we can do that competitors can't). Flag known limitations or constraints that Sales needs to disclose proactively to avoid objections later. Include which geographies and segments are in scope at launch.

**Pricing, terms, and special cases**
2–3 bullet points. Confirm pricing tier, whether any special arrangements (pilot pricing, introductory offers) are available at launch, and any customer segments that may be excluded from this offering.

Tone: energetic, commercial, confident. Keep the full note under 400 words.
```

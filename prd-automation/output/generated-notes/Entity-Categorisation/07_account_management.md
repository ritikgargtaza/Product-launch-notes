# Account Management — Release Notes: Entity Categorisation

## What's new

Existing OBO merchants now have a **risk category** assigned (A = low-risk, B = medium-risk, C = high-risk). We're also tracking what type of merchant they are (Technical Service Provider vs Payment Service Provider) and any licenses they hold. Compliance can update a merchant's category or type at any time. This is an internal classification system — merchants don't see it on their dashboard, but it affects how quickly their payouts are approved.

## What's in it for you

**Churn risk mitigation:** Merchants in category B or C may experience hard-stopped payouts initially. Proactive outreach is critical: brief top accounts that hard-stops are a temporary compliance gate, not a service degradation. Frame it as "we're verifying your use case for the fastest possible approval."

**Upsell opportunity:** Category A merchants can expand OBO volume without additional compliance review. Merchants still in B or C have an upgrade path — they can work with our compliance team to move to A and unlock higher velocity. Use this as part of your renewal pitch.

**Expansion conversations:** Merchants expanding from **just payouts to payouts + collections** may need full KYB on the collection flow. Coordinate with compliance to understand the new requirements before you discuss expansion with them.
